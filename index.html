Where to Start!!!

DELHI

Delhi is the largest city in India in terms of geographic size, with the Yamuna being the only river that passes through it. It is home to the largest and most rapidly growing retail industries of the nation. Delhi has a rich historical significance due to its magnificent tombs, temples, mausoleums, monuments, and ancient forts and fortresses. Hindu kings and Mughal rulers made Delhi their capital city.

MUMBAI

Mumbai is India's largest city and one of the world's most populous cities, with a population of over 23 million people living under the Brihanmumbai Municipal Corporation.It is the financial capital of India and a global city, with many billionaires living there. Mumbai generates more than 6% of India's GDP and accounts for 25% of industrial output, 40% of sea trade, and 70% of capital transactions to India's economy.

KOLKATA

Kolkata, India's second biggest city, is a perpetually ongoing festival of human existence, concurrently luxurious and squalid, refined and frantic, pointedly futuristic, while beautifully in decay. A vibrant 350-year-old metropolis located on India's Eastern Coast, the capital of West Bengal thrives on contradictions and imposing spectacles; nothing is commonplace in this city. Famously known as the City of Joy, Kolkata is, in every sense, the artistic, cultural and intellectual capital of the country.
